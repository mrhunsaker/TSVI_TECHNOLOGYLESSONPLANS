Section 1.19 Miscellaneous: Use of the Oxford Dictionary

  1. You can open the dictionary/thesaurus from anywhere on the BrailleNote.

    1. Open Options Menu

    2. Select "Look up" or press l

    3. "Use dictionary or thesaurus?" is the cue.

    4. Press d for dictionary.

    5. "Word to look up in dictionary?" is the cue.

    6. Write the word you want to look up.

    7. Press enter.

    8. You can use the back or advance thumb-key to read the braille.

  2. Other available commands in dictionary:

    1. For information about the word space + i

    2. Pronounce word (speech on) enter + dots25

    3. Move to the next entry space + dots56

    4. More to previous entry space + dots23

    5. Insert word under cursor backspace + i

    6. Copy entry to clipboard backspace + k

    7. Look up another word space + e

  3. Use dictionary from within a document:

    1. Open your document.

    2. Place the cursor on the word you want to look up.

    3. Press space + o.

    4. Press letter l for "look up".

    5. Press d for dictionary.

    6. Notice the word your cursor was on in the document is already displayed after the "word to look up" cue.

    7. Press enter to look up the word.

    8. Use dictionary commands to read.

  4. Move forward or back by entry:

    1. Let's say you look up the word "bobber". You are on that word in the entry and can read the definition.

    2. To go to the next entry space + dots56

    3. To go back the other way space + 23.

    4. To move back and forward by word when reading the definition, use space + dot2 or space + dot5.
