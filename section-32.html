Section 2.9 Email

Skills Addressed in this Lesson:

  • Send an attachment.

  • Use the address selection list to attain a recipient e-mail address.

  • Access and understand the e-mail action menu.

Subsection 2.9.1 Tasks

This lesson assumes that you have addresses in your BrailleNote address book that contain e-mail addresses.

  1. Instruct your student to open Keymail.

  2. Space or use the next thumb-key to locate "write an e-mail". Press enter.

  3. At the "send to" prompt, press backspace with dots1 2 3 to get to the "selection list for address" prompt.

  4. Your student should move to the field of his or her choice. For this lesson, first or last name will probably be best. On the desired field, write the name of the recipient you want to e-mail and press enter.

  5. "One record selected. Send to all recipients?" will be the cue. Press a y for yes. At this time you and your student will be taken to the "also send to" field.

  6. If you would like your student to send the message to someone else, either write the e-mail address or use backspace with dots1 2 3 to go to the address selection option.

  7. The next step to assist your student with is to move through the e-mail fields. At the subject field, your student should get in the habit of writing a one or two word descriptive subject. Then use the next thumb-key to get to the attachment field.

  8. The BrailleNote user is then given the question, "attach a file to this e-mail?" Your student should press a y for yes to gain practice in attaching a file to an e-mail message.

  9. The next prompt is "folder name". Use space or the next thumb-key to go to the folder where there is a keyword file stored that you can attach for practice. Press enter.

  10. "File to attach?" is the next question. Use the next thumb-key or space to the file you want to attach and press enter.

  11. "Attach this keyword document in another file type?" is the next cue. Key in a y for yes. Microsoft Word is a common file type that most computer users can access. For now let's attach the file as a Microsoft Word document.

  12. "Attach as which file type?" is the next question. Your display may already say "Microsoft Word." Press enter to attach the file as a Microsoft Word document.

  13. Now your student will be asked if he or she would like to attach another file. Write an n for no at this point.

  14. Your student will find the e-mail message. He or she may write a message. A note about the file that is attached and the reason for it would be very appropriate and polite. When your student is finished writing the message, he or she may press space with e.

  15. "Ready to send this message?" If the message is good to go, your student should press y. "Save a copy?" can probably be responded to with an n for no at this time. As you leave Keymail, you will be prompted to send the message. Key in a y for yes or n for no.

  16. Now it's time for your student and you to learn about the e-mail action menu. This feature comes in handy in cases where the BrailleNote user wants to forward an e-mail message, reply to an e-mail message, or move an e-mail message to a different folder.

  17. When you are focused on or in an e-mail message, you can access the e-mail action menu by pressing space with dots2 and 6. "E-mail action menu" will be spoken or displayed. The choices in the e-mail action menu are:

    • Reply

    • Forward

    • Move e-mail to another folder

    • Copy this e-mail to another folder

    • Delete this e-mail from this folder

    • Print this e-mail

    • Emboss this e-mail

    • All. Mark all e-mail

  18. Encourage your student to access e-mail through the BrailleNote in order to use the options within the e-mail action menu. Enter on the choice that you want to open.

  19. Return to the Main Menu at this time.
