Section 2.13 Scientific Calculator

Skills Addressed in this Lesson:

  • Enter the BrailleNote scientific calculator from the Main Menu.

  • Enter the scientific calculator by using the shortcut key.

  • Complete division, multiplication, addition, and subtraction problems by using the calculator.

  • Use decimal points if they are needed.

  • Get help from within the scientific calculator.

  • Insert a calculation into a word document.

  • Quickly switch between the scientific calculator and a keyword document.

  • Exit the calculator.

Subsection 2.13.1 Tasks

Adjust this lesson according to the grade and ability level of your student.

  1. From the Main Menu, instruct your student to space until scientific calculator is spoken or displayed. Press enter to open the calculator.

  2. On the Braille display, your student will feel the number 0.

  3. Use e with space to return to the Main Menu. A full cell with space will also get you back to the Main Menu.

  4. Ask your student to explain another way in which the scientific calculator can be opened from the Main Menu. Yes, press the letter s for scientific calculator to open the application. When the calculator is open, do not exit to the Main Menu this time.

  5. Press backspace with enter with w to open the keyword application.

  6. Encourage your student to create a document in a folder called Math.

  7. Create a folder if necessary by writing the word math at the "folder to open" cue. When asked if you want to create the folder, press y for yes. Make a new file within the folder called "practice 1" for use during this lesson. Again, just write the file name when prompted to do so. Answer y for yes when asked if you want to create the file. Give your student help as needed.

  8. At the top of the blank document, your student may write a heading.

  9. The first line may contain the student's name. Follow this with a new line by pressing the enter key.

  10. Your student may write the date on the second line.

  11. The third line of the document can contain the assignment name "Math Practice." Notice that both words of the assignment name are capitalized.

  12. Many teachers require students to include a heading on assignments and tests. Students who are blind or visually impaired should not be exempt from requirements that their nondisabled peers must abide by.

  13. Follow the heading with two new lines. Your student should write 1 followed by a period and a space to get ready for the first math problem.

  14. The shortcut for opening the calculator is enter with backspace with

  15. s. Your student should do this now.

  16. In the calculator, you do not need to use the numeric indicator "the number sign." You or your student may use upper or lower case numbers.

  17. Nemeth Code users may want to practice dropping their numbers while using the scientific calculator to remain in the habit of using Braille math.

  18. Help your student complete a simple addition problem. The addition sign is dots3 4 6. To obtain a result, press the enter key.

  19. Now your student may clear the calculator by pushing backspace with dots1 4.

  20. Try a subtraction problem. The subtraction sign is dots3 6. Use a variety of numbers so that your student can practice inputting numbers into the scientific calculator.

  21. After clearing the result, try some multiplication problems. The multiplication sign is dots1 6.

  22. The division sign is dots3 4. If you need a decimal point, use dots4 6.

  23. At any time you may get extensive help from within the scientific calculator. Press space with h. Your student should try this now.

  24. In the calculator help menu, your student can space to the areas of General Commands, Basic Functions, Fractions, Memory Commands, Scientific Functions, and Statistics.

  25. Enter on any item to read more about it. Your student should explore commands and information in the scientific calculator help menu to learn specific commands and tasks based on his or her grade or ability level. The BrailleNote gives terrific help from anywhere. You and your student can learn an awesome amount of information by taking advantage of this feature from anywhere on the BrailleNote.

  26. Use e with space to go back by level until you get back to the scientific calculator.

  27. At the scientific calculator entry point, complete an addition problem, then get the result. Do not clear the answer this time.

  28. Press enter with backspace with w to return to the document you created earlier in this lesson.

  29. Ensure that your cursor is placed after number 1.

  30. Insert your calculator result into your document. Press enter with i.

  31. Then press c for calculation.

Subsection 2.13.2 Quiz

Write or say the best answer to each question.

  1. What is the shortcut to get to the scientific calculator from within a keyword document?

  2. Which dotsmake the minus sign?

  3. How can you clear the calculator?

  4. From Main Menu, what letter should you press to get to the scientific calculator?

  5. When using the calculator, what are dots4 6 used for?

  6. To get started with inserting a calculation, what key combination should you press?

  7. How can you exit the calculator?

  8. What will dots1 and 6 do within the calculator?

  9. To get help from within the calculator, what key combination should you press?

  10. The BrailleNote calculator is not scientific.
