Section 2.3 Word Processing

Skills Addressed in this Lesson:

  • Open the word processor.

  • Create a document.

  • Write a heading and two sentences in a document.

  • Use the backspace and cursor keys to make basic corrections.

  • Review text moving forward and back by character and word.

  • Review text backward and forward by sentence and paragraph.

  • Use the back and advance thumb-keys to move back and forward by one width of the Braille display.

  • Turn the speech on and off.

  • Turn the Braille display on and off.

  • Determine how much power is left and become aware of when the BrailleNote needs to be charged.

Subsection 2.3.1 Tasks

  1. Turn on the BrailleNote on/off rocker switch

  2. Go to Main Menu space + dots123456

  3. From the Main Menu, open Keyword space, enter

  4. In KeyWord Menu, create a document space, enter or letter c

  5. Choose Folder for Document space, enter

  6. Name the file by typing a name (\lt250 characters), then press enter

    1. When in the document, instruct your student to write:

      1. Their name, then enter

      2. The date, then enter

      3. The word "Practice''

      4. Presenter twice to place a blank line after "Practice''

      5. Now your student may write two or three sentences to get a feel for what writing in the word processor is like.

    2. Move Cursor Location cursor routing keys

    3. Delete text backspace key

  7. Listed below are commands that can be used on the BrailleNote. The current document will be too small to practice them all, but a quick review won't hurt. Review commands are especially helpful when the speech is being used.

    1. To move back by character space + dot3

    2. To move forward by character space + dot6

    3. To move back by word space + dot2

    4. To move forward by word space + dot5

    5. To move back by sentence space + dot1

    6. To move forward by sentence space + dot4

    7. To move back b paragraph space + dots23

    8. To move forward by paragraph space + dots56

    9. To move back the width of 1 braille display back thumb-key

  8. To move back the width of 1 braille display advance thumb-key

  9. Turn braille display on/off space + next thumb-key

  10. Access Options Menu space + o thumb-key

    1. See Power/battery level letter p

    2. Clear power level and return to document next thumb-key

Subsection 2.3.2 Quiz

Your student may answer the following true and false questions. Then, you may require them to demonstrate knowledge of the skills learned in this lesson by actually using the BrailleNote.

  1. It is not necessary to check the BrailleNote power level.

  2. Using the space with dot4 will go forward by a sentence.

  3. The previous thumb-key with space will turn the Braille display on and off.

  4. Space with dot2 will move forward by character in the word processor.

  5. File names can be up to 350 characters long.

  6. Space with the previous thumb-key turns the speech on and off.

  7. Space with dot6 moves forward by character.

  8. Space with o will get you to the options menu.

  9. After checking the power level, a user must first return to the Main Menu to reset the BrailleNote.

  10. Keyword is the name of the BrailleNote word processor.
