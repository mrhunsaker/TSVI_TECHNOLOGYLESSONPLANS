Section 2.1 Getting Started

Skills Addressed in this Lesson:

Learn where the BrailleNote Apex keys are located.

  • Six Braille writer keys

  • Space

  • Backspace

  • Enter

  • Previous thumb-key

  • Back thumb-key

  • Advance thumb-key

  • Next thumb-key

  • on and off switch

  • Headphone jack

Subsection 2.1.1 Tasks

  1. Ask your student to open the cover of the BrailleNote

    • The cover should open away from the BrailleNote user

    • If the BrailleNote is positioned correctly, your student will recognize six Braille writer keys and a spacebar which are similar to that of a Perkins Braille writer

  2. Assist your student in locating the on off switch located under the velcro flap toward the back of the left panel of the BrailleNote

  3. Before turning the BrailleNote on

    • Explain that when the rocker switch is indented toward the user, the BrailleNote is on

    • When the on off switch is indented away from the user, the BrailleNote is of

  4. Allow your student to feel the headphone jack on the left panel of the BrailleNote toward the front.

  5. Instruct your BrailleNote user to place his or her hands on the six Braille writer keys as he or she would do on a Braille writer.

  6. The key near the left edge of the BrailleNote, to the left of the dot3 Braille writer key, is the backspace key.

  7. The key next to the right edge of the BrailleNote, to the right of the dot6 Braille writer key, is the enter key.

  8. The refreshable Braille display is below the spacebar.

  9. While your student is exploring the refreshable Braille display, he or she may place both thumbs down on the thumb-keys to explore them. The thumb-keys are on the front panel of the BrailleNote.

  10. The thumb-keys are, starting at the left:

    • previous

    • back

    • advance

    • next thumb-keys.

Subsection 2.1.2 Quiz

Have your student point to each of the following:

  1. Earphone jack

  2. Spacebar

  3. Next thumb-key

  4. on and off rocker switch

  5. Previous thumb-key

  6. Backspace

  7. Advance thumb-key

  8. Back thumb-key

  9. Return (enter) key

  10. Keys one through six
